# Anonymous product analytics with a privacy opt-out

- Date: `2026-05-01`
- Channel: `nightly`
- Version: `0.1.0-nightly.426.1`
- Release: https://github.com/cypher-asi/aura-os/releases/tag/v0.1.0-nightly.426.1

Today's nightly wires Aura into Mixpanel for anonymous, privacy-respecting product analytics. Tracking spans authentication, chat, AURA 3D, projects, agents, tasks, processes, integrations, notes, and billing — and ships alongside a new Privacy section in Settings where anyone can opt out.

## 2:31 AM — Mixpanel analytics rollout with Privacy settings opt-out

Aura's interface gains an end-to-end product analytics layer built on Mixpanel, paired with a user-facing Privacy panel and TypeScript fixes to keep the build green.

- Introduced an anonymous-by-default Mixpanel pipeline that tracks app open, sign-up, login, and chat sends with model metadata, honors DNT/GPC browser signals, supports localStorage opt-out, and stays fully disabled when VITE_MIXPANEL_TOKEN is unset. (`f134511`)
- Expanded event coverage across the product surface — projects, agents, tasks, processes, AURA 3D image and model generation, marketplace hires, integrations, notes, feedback, settings/tier modal opens, and invite-code copies — plus chat-side events for model selection, agent selection, file attachments (with count), and session resets across all three reset paths. (`a8c653d`, `edeb438`)
- Added subscription and credit-purchase tracking (plan and amount) and shipped a new Privacy section in Org Settings with a one-click opt-in/opt-out toggle for anonymous usage data. (`468f979`)
- Polished the Privacy panel layout — moving the longer disclosure copy into a section intro paragraph beneath the toggle — and resolved TypeScript build errors in the analytics platform-detection cast and the project-creation event's orbit_repo field. (`977a0f7`, `bdee6c5`)

## Highlights
